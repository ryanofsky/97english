Transcendentalism goes against the philosophy of most public and private schools in our society. Our schools try to give everybody the same education. That's why every class uses the same textbooks and does the same projects and learns in the same order. On a larger scale, this is also why there is such a push for national standardization and testing. In a transcendental school, every student would receive a different education, depending on interests and strength. Also, public schools prefer a broad education with a little bit of everything. The Mighty Paper Clip

I must confess that when I walked into my English class last Tuesday the object I had brought to share my memories and feelings for, the object that would reveal the meaning of life, the object I had chosen, was only a paper clip. Of course, I had plans to find something more suitable the next day. But during that class period I began to like the idea a little more. The problem was I still could not think of any sort of memory, experience, or interesting thing to say about it.

So that night, I went right over to the Camp Hill Mall. There is this lady who hangs out there in the arcade who is some kind of drug dealer / psychic. I don’t do drugs, but I was hoping that maybe I could use her psychic abilities to recover some lost memories about paper clips. So she took me to her office, and tried out several treatments. She hypnotized me, tried some electric shock therapy, and put me in front of this strange machine with mysterious sounds and lights. Actually, I think she was just playing Sonic the Hedgehog, but I was unable to tell without my glasses. Anyway, none of it seemed to work. But suddenly, when she was on the phone with some sort of "unlicensed brain surgeon", the memories came flooding back, and tears came to my eyes… I will share one of these memories here.

When I was 5 years old, I didn’t go to public school but to some tiny religious school in Harrisburg. Since there were only three or four people from this school who lived on the West Shore, we got to ride on one of those vans that say "School Students," instead of a bus. And except for the fact that the radio was constantly blaring "Tim and Sue and the Wink Morning Zoo," it was not bad. But one day, when we were on a bridge over the Susquehanna river, something happened and the car swerved to the side of the road. Before we even realized what was going on, the car had flown over the guardrail and we were in midair over the river. Thinking quickly, I took a long chain of paper clips out of my bookbag, fastened one end to a seat, and threw the other end out of the window, and up to the bridge. Luckily for us it caught some other section of the guardrail. But our troubles were not over. Although the paper clip chain was more than capable of supporting the weight of the van, the guardrail was creaking and was about to crumble. Once again, I sprung into action. I climbed up into the front of the van, shattered the windshield with my fist, and ascended up the long chain of paper clips. Once I was at the top, I took hold of the chain, and used every bit of my strength to pull the van back up to safety. I had not only saved my own life but also the lives of three others, and all thanks to paper clips.

But now that I look back on that incident, I now understand that I owe more to paper clips than just my life. Thanks to them, and this English class, I have come to believe that the hidden meaning, the ultimate purpose in our lives is in fact:

SCOTTISH HIGHLAND DANCING.

You may be skeptical now, but read on. Soon, you too will see how no life is complete without beloved highland dances like "Strathspey and Reels", "Wilt Thou go the Barracks, Johnnie?" and "The Highland Laddie." Let me explain how I came upon this ultimate truth. I noticed that when rearranged, the letters in PAPER CLIP spell PA CRIPPLE, CAP PER LIP, and CLAP PIPER. While most of these combinations are probably pure coincidence, I had a hunch that CLAP PIPER would lead me to the truth. I thought about it for a very long time. CLAP and PIPER, what was the connection, and what was the meaning of life? But I couldn’t think of anything, and I decided I need a break. So, I decided to relax and visit The Minnesota Scottish Celtic Dance Association Home Page to catch up on all the latest news and read some more fun facts about those crazy highland dances. But little did I know that the connection between CLAP and PIPER and the path that would lead me to the ultimate meaning of life was right before my eyes. The sentence read: "The clap is a signal from the dancer to the piper that the piper is supposed to change tempo for a quick step." "Of course," I thought, "the meaning of life is to go Scottish Highland Dancing!" The ultimate meaning of life had been lost in the seeming simplicity of the paper clip, perhaps since ancient times, but I had uncovered it!!

I am so happy that I’ve had this time to explore the greatness of the paper clip. The time I’ve spend working on this has been the most fulfilling and rewarding experience of my life. I’ve come to see paper clips as more than a twisty piece of metal that comes in many colors and sizes. Paper clips are our wiry friends that attach one piece of paper to another, save lives, and have so much to teach us. Stop reading, Right Now and Hug a Paper Clip.
